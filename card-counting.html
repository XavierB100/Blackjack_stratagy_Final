Card Counting Guide

Advanced techniques for tracking the deck composition

Disclaimer: Card counting is legal but casinos may ask you to leave. This information is for educational purposes only.

What is Card Counting?

Card counting is a technique used to track the ratio of high to low cards remaining in the deck. When more high cards (10s, face cards, Aces) remain, the player has a statistical advantage.

Why High Cards Favor the Player:

  • More blackjacks (pays 3:2)
  • Dealer busts more often on stiff hands
  • Better double down opportunities
  • More profitable splitting situations

Hi-Lo Counting System

The Hi-Lo system is the most popular and effective card counting method for beginners. Each card is assigned a value:

Low Cards (+1)

2 3 4 5 6

Neutral Cards (0)

7 8 9

High Cards (-1)

10 J Q K A

Running Count vs True Count

Running Count

The ongoing tally of all cards seen since the shuffle. Simply add or subtract card values as they appear.

Example: Cards dealt: 2, K, 5, 7, A, 3
Running Count: +1 -1 +1 +0 -1 +1 = +1

True Count

The running count divided by the estimated number of decks remaining. This normalizes the count across different deck sizes.

Formula: True Count = Running Count รท Decks Remaining
Example: Running Count +6, 3 decks left
True Count: +6 รท 3 = +2

Strategy Deviations

When the true count is significantly positive or negative, basic strategy should be modified. Here are the most important deviations:

Insurance (True Count +3 or higher)

Take insurance when the true count is +3 or higher, as there are more 10-value cards remaining.

16 vs 10 (True Count 0 or higher)

Stand on 16 vs dealer 10 when true count is 0 or higher (instead of hitting).

15 vs 10 (True Count +4 or higher)

Stand on 15 vs dealer 10 when true count is +4 or higher.

12 vs 2 (True Count +3 or higher)

Stand on 12 vs dealer 2 when true count is +3 or higher.

Heat Management & Casino Countermeasures

Avoiding Detection

Bet Spread Management

Keep your bet spread reasonable. A 1-8 spread is generally safe for most games. Avoid dramatic bet increases.

Good: $25 โ $50 โ $100 โ $150
Bad: $25 โ $25 โ $300 โ $25

Playing Deviations

Use basic strategy deviations sparingly and only for the most profitable situations.

High Priority:
  • Insurance at +3 true count
  • 16 vs 10 stand at 0 true count
Lower Priority:
  • 15 vs 10 stand at +4
  • 12 vs 2 stand at +3

Table Behavior

Act like a typical recreational player. Avoid obvious counting behaviors.

Do:
  • Make casual conversation
  • Tip the dealer occasionally
  • Take breaks naturally
  • Order drinks (non-alcoholic)
Don't:
  • Stare at cards intensely
  • Move lips while counting
  • Dramatically change bet sizes
  • Leave immediately after shuffles

Common Casino Countermeasures

Preferential Shuffling

Shuffling when the count becomes favorable to players. Kills your advantage.

Response: Find tables with better penetration or leave.

Flat Betting

Dealer may ask you to keep bets consistent or limit your betting spread.

Response: Comply or find another table/casino.

Back-off

Casino asks you to stop playing blackjack but allows other games.

Response: Leave politely. You may be in their database.

Trespass

Casino bans you completely from the property.

Response: Leave immediately. Don't return to that casino chain.

Important Considerations

Bankroll Management

Card counting requires a substantial bankroll to weather the natural variance. A good rule is to have at least 100-200 times your maximum bet.

Casino Countermeasures

Casinos use various methods to detect counters: surveillance, frequent shuffling, betting pattern analysis, and barring suspected counters.

Practice Requirements

Successful card counting requires hundreds of hours of practice to maintain accuracy under casino conditions.
