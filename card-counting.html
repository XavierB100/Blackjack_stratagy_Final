Card Counting Guide

Advanced techniques for tracking the deck composition

Disclaimer: Card counting is legal but casinos may ask you to leave. This information is for educational purposes only.

What is Card Counting?

Card counting is a technique used to track the ratio of high to low cards remaining in the deck. When more high cards (10s, face cards, Aces) remain, the player has a statistical advantage.

Why High Cards Favor the Player:

  • More blackjacks (pays 3:2)
  • Dealer busts more often on stiff hands
  • Better double down opportunities
  • More profitable splitting situations

Hi-Lo Counting System

The Hi-Lo system is the most popular and effective card counting method for beginners. Each card is assigned a value:

Low Cards (+1)

2 3 4 5 6

Neutral Cards (0)

7 8 9

High Cards (-1)

10 J Q K A

Running Count vs True Count

Running Count

The ongoing tally of all cards seen since the shuffle. Simply add or subtract card values as they appear.

Example: Cards dealt: 2, K, 5, 7, A, 3
Running Count: +1 -1 +1 +0 -1 +1 = +1

True Count

The running count divided by the estimated number of decks remaining. This normalizes the count across different deck sizes.

Formula: True Count = Running Count ÷ Decks Remaining
Example: Running Count +6, 3 decks left
True Count: +6 ÷ 3 = +2

Betting Strategy

Adjust your bet size based on the true count to maximize profits when you have the advantage:

True Count Bet Size Player Advantage Action
+1 or less Minimum -0.5% to 0% Basic strategy, minimum bet
+2 2x minimum +0.5% Slight advantage, increase bet
+3 3x minimum +1.0% Good advantage, larger bet
+4 4x minimum +1.5% Strong advantage, maximum bet
+5 or more 5x minimum +2.0%+ Very strong advantage, max bet

Strategy Deviations

When the true count is significantly positive or negative, basic strategy should be modified. Here are the most important deviations:

Insurance (True Count +3 or higher)

Take insurance when the true count is +3 or higher, as there are more 10-value cards remaining.

16 vs 10 (True Count 0 or higher)

Stand on 16 vs dealer 10 when true count is 0 or higher (instead of hitting).

15 vs 10 (True Count +4 or higher)

Stand on 15 vs dealer 10 when true count is +4 or higher.

12 vs 2 (True Count +3 or higher)

Stand on 12 vs dealer 2 when true count is +3 or higher.

True Count Calculation

Why True Count is Critical

The True Count is the heart of professional card counting, converting your running count into actionable intelligence. While the running count tells you what's happened, the true count tells you your actual advantage and how to bet accordingly. This drill transforms you from a card observer into a strategic player with mathematical precision.

Essential Skills Developed

  • Mathematical Precision: Master accurate division under pressure without calculators or obvious hesitation
  • Deck Estimation: Develop the ability to quickly and accurately estimate remaining decks in the shoe
  • Strategic Decision Making: Learn when true count justifies increasing bets or changing basic strategy
  • Risk Assessment: Understand how true count directly correlates to your mathematical edge
  • Mental Calculation Speed: Process true count conversions instantly without disrupting game flow

The Mathematics Behind True Count

The Problem: A running count of +6 means very different things in different situations:

  • +6 with 6 decks remaining = +1 true count (minimal advantage)
  • +6 with 1 deck remaining = +6 true count (huge advantage!)

The Solution: True Count = Running Count ÷ Decks Remaining

This normalization allows you to make consistent betting and playing decisions regardless of how many cards have been played.

When True Count Matters Most

Betting Decisions

True count determines bet size. Each +1 in true count typically justifies increasing your bet by your base unit. Never increase bets based on running count alone.

Insurance Bets

Take insurance only when true count reaches +3 or higher. This is when the probability of dealer blackjack exceeds the 30% needed to make insurance profitable.

Basic Strategy Deviations

Modify basic strategy only when true count reaches specific thresholds. For example, stand on 16 vs 10 when true count is 0 or higher.

Critical Errors to Avoid

Rounding Errors in Division

Problem: Imprecise division leading to incorrect true count calculations.
Solution: Practice mental division to nearest 0.5. For example, +7 ÷ 3 decks = +2.33, round to +2.5 true count.

Poor Deck Estimation

Problem: Inaccurate estimation of remaining decks in the shoe.
Solution: Learn to visually estimate deck thickness. Practice with physical cards to calibrate your estimation skills.

Betting on Running Count

Problem: Increasing bets based on running count instead of true count.
Solution: Never make betting decisions without first calculating true count. High running count late in the shoe is far more valuable than early in the shoe.

Effective Practice Approach

  • Start Simple: Begin with easy divisions (even numbers, common fractions)
  • Build Complexity: Progress to more challenging scenarios with decimal remainders
  • Speed Development: Practice until calculations become automatic and instant
  • Real-World Scenarios: Focus on common casino situations (4-6 deck games, typical penetration)
  • Accuracy Emphasis: Perfect accuracy is more important than speed - wrong true count leads to wrong decisions

Mastery Indicators

  • Calculation Speed: Convert running count to true count in under 2 seconds
  • Accuracy Standard: 95%+ accuracy on varied scenarios
  • Decimal Precision: Comfortable with calculations to nearest 0.5
  • Deck Estimation: Estimate remaining decks within ±0.5 decks consistently
  • Strategic Application: Know exactly what each true count level means for betting and playing decisions

The Mathematics of Advantage Betting

True Count to Betting Units Conversion

Basic Rule: Bet your minimum unit for every positive true count above +1

  • True Count +1 or less: 1 unit (minimum bet)
  • True Count +2: 2 units
  • True Count +3: 3 units
  • True Count +4 or more: 4+ units (table maximum consideration)

Example: $25 minimum bet, True Count +3 = $75 bet

Bankroll Requirements

Professional Standard: 100-200 times your maximum bet in bankroll

  • Conservative: 200x max bet (1% risk of ruin)
  • Moderate: 150x max bet (2-3% risk of ruin)
  • Aggressive: 100x max bet (5-10% risk of ruin)

Example: $100 max bet requires $15,000-$20,000 bankroll

Heat Management & Casino Countermeasures

Avoiding Detection

Bet Spread Management

Keep your bet spread reasonable. A 1-8 spread is generally safe for most games. Avoid dramatic bet increases.

Good: $25 → $50 → $100 → $150
Bad: $25 → $25 → $300 → $25

Playing Deviations

Use basic strategy deviations sparingly and only for the most profitable situations.

High Priority:
  • Insurance at +3 true count
  • 16 vs 10 stand at 0 true count
Lower Priority:
  • 15 vs 10 stand at +4
  • 12 vs 2 stand at +3

Table Behavior

Act like a typical recreational player. Avoid obvious counting behaviors.

Do:
  • Make casual conversation
  • Tip the dealer occasionally
  • Take breaks naturally
  • Order drinks (non-alcoholic)
Don't:
  • Stare at cards intensely
  • Move lips while counting
  • Dramatically change bet sizes
  • Leave immediately after shuffles

Common Casino Countermeasures

Preferential Shuffling

Shuffling when the count becomes favorable to players. Kills your advantage.

Response: Find tables with better penetration or leave.

Flat Betting

Dealer may ask you to keep bets consistent or limit your betting spread.

Response: Comply or find another table/casino.

Back-off

Casino asks you to stop playing blackjack but allows other games.

Response: Leave politely. You may be in their database.

Trespass

Casino bans you completely from the property.

Response: Leave immediately. Don't return to that casino chain.
